# §8e — Xeroriparian planting palette + calendar (Candasnos / Valcuerna, Los Monegros)
_Verified native palette for the arid Ebro / gypsum-saline steppe (~350 mm/yr). Feeds §8e planting calendar, §6 masterplan, §8 phased sections. Draft by Claude; Sonya/teacher to confirm before use. Species chosen for local provenance, drought + gypsum + salt tolerance, erosion control, and food-web value for the rabbit → lynx chain._

## Design principle
Match species to the physical gradient of the barranco, not a generic "green" planting. Five bands run from the wet channel out to the dry matrix, matching the three strategy zones (Left Ravine / Agricultural Crossing / River Interface). Woody planting concentrates in the moisture-collecting lines (channel, north-facing slopes, water-harvesting microcatchments); the gypsum crests and salt flats are left mostly to specialist colonisers. This is an arid mosaic, deliberately sparse, not a riparian gallery forest.

---
## 1. Riparian core  (barranco floor, River Interface zone)
Moisture-seeking, flood- and drought-tolerant. The green thread of the corridor.

| Species | Common (ES) | Habit | Tolerances | Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| *Fraxinus angustifolia* | Fresno | small tree | ravine-bottom water, seasonal flood | canopy, shade, bank hold |
| *Populus alba* / *P. nigra* | Álamo / Chopo | tree | high water table (river interface only) | canopy, cuttings-easy |
| *Salix purpurea* / *S. neotricha* | Sauce | shrub/tree | channel edge, flood | bank stabilisation, fast cuttings |
| *Tamarix gallica* / *T. africana* / *T. canariensis* | Taray | shrub/tree | salt + drought, sandy dry reaches | cover on saline banks |

⚠ Use only the **native Iberian tamarisks** above. Do NOT plant the invasive American *Tamarix ramosissima*.
⚠ *Nerium oleander* (adelfa) suits dry watercourses but is frost-sensitive; risky in the continental Monegros winter, use only in sheltered lower reaches if at all.

## 2. Xeric shrub matrix  (lower slopes, hedgerows on the Agricultural Crossing zone)
The structural backbone: cover, nitrogen, and fauna refugia. This band carries the corridor argument.

| Species | Common (ES) | Habit | Tolerances | Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| *Ziziphus lotus* | Arto / Azufaifo | mound shrub | extreme drought, heat | **keystone** — builds fertile mounds, rabbit-warren refugia (→ lynx prey), fruit |
| *Retama sphaerocarpa* | Retama | tall broom | drought, poor soil | **N-fixer**, pioneer cover, nurse plant |
| *Rhamnus lycioides* | Espino negro | thorny shrub | drought | facilitator/nurse, dense cover |
| *Quercus coccifera* | Coscoja | shrub | drought | facilitator, evergreen cover |
| *Pistacia lentiscus* | Lentisco | shrub | drought (mild frost limit) | cover, berries; nurse species |
| *Atriplex halimus* | Orgaza / Salado | shrub | salt + drought | fast establishment, forage, quick cover |

## 3. Gypsum badland specialists  (yesos crests and slopes — 20% of the box)
Gypsophytes for the gypsum substrate that generic species cannot hold. Mostly direct-seeded or left to colonise; use *Gypsophila* as a nurse.

| Species | Common (ES) | Role |
|---|---|---|
| *Gypsophila struthium* | Asnallo / Jabonera | gypsum **nurse plant** for recovery of degraded gypsum |
| *Ononis tridentata* | Garbancillo | Iberian endemic gypsophyte, slope hold |
| *Lepidium subulatum* | — | gypsophyte, early coloniser of gypsum crusts |
| *Helianthemum squamatum* | Jarilla de escamas | gypsophyte, ground cover |
| *Herniaria fruticosa*, *Thymus* spp. | — / Tomillo | gypsum sub-shrub cover |

## 4. Saline flats / saladas fringe  (endorheic basins)
**Mostly do not plant.** Let halophytes colonise naturally and protect the hydrology; salt zonation self-organises by tolerance. Palette for reference / fringe reinforcement only.

| Species | Common (ES) | Salt zone |
|---|---|---|
| *Arthrocnemum macrostachyum* / *Sarcocornia* | Sosa jabonera | wettest, highest salt |
| *Suaeda vera* | Sosa | high salt |
| *Salsola vermiculata* | Sisallo | fringe, forage |
| *Limonium* spp. | Siempreviva / Saladilla | mid fringe |
| *Frankenia thymifolia* | Tomillo sapero | crust edge |

## 5. Steppe ground layer + aromatics  (matrix, erosion control everywhere)
Grasses and sub-shrubs for slope stabilisation, the sparse matrix, and pollinators. Direct-seeded.

| Species | Common (ES) | Role |
|---|---|---|
| *Lygeum spartum* | Albardín | **pioneer**, erosion control, salt + gypsum tolerant |
| *Macrochloa tenacissima* (*Stipa t.*) | Esparto / Atocha | steppe grass, slope stabilisation |
| *Stipa parviflora* | — | ground cover |
| *Salvia rosmarinus* (*Rosmarinus*) | Romero | aromatic cover, pollinators |
| *Thymus vulgaris* / *T. zygis* | Tomillo | aromatic sub-shrub |
| *Artemisia herba-alba* | Ontina | steppe sub-shrub, forage |

## Planting calendar (species × operation × month)
Mediterranean winter-rainfall regime: plant into the cool, moist window so roots establish before summer drought. Germination optimum ~13–18 °C. **Plant Oct–Feb; avoid Apr–Sep.**

```
Operation \ Month        J  F  M  A  M  J  J  A  S  O  N  D
Seed collection (grasses)               ●  ●              ○      → Lygeum, Stipa/Macrochloa (early summer)
Seed collection (shrubs/fruit)                      ○  ●  ●  ○   → Retama, Ziziphus, gypsophytes (late summer–autumn)
Hardwood cuttings (Salix/Populus/Tamarix) ●  ●                          ●  → dormant season
Container planting-out (all woody)     ●  ●                    ○  ●  ●  → PEAK Nov–Jan
Direct seeding (grasses/gypsum/aromatic)                     ●  ●  ○   → autumn, onto winter rain
Establishment watering (year 1 only)              ○  ●  ●  ○         → only worst sites, then wean off
Do NOT plant                        .  .  ✗  ✗  ✗  ✗  ✗  ✗  .  .  .  .
```
● primary window · ○ secondary. Cuttings taken in dormancy (Dec–Feb) can be stuck immediately in the channel band.

## Technique notes (semi-arid reality)
- **Expect losses.** Semi-arid plantings survive poorly (e.g. *Pistacia lentiscus* ~36% at 3 years in SE Iberia). Plan replacement planting, and prefer **container stock over direct seeding** for the woody species.
- **Use nurse plants + facilitation.** Establish *Retama*, *Rhamnus lycioides*, *Quercus coccifera* and (on gypsum) *Gypsophila struthium* first; plant sensitive species in their shade.
- **Harvest water, don't irrigate.** Tie planting to the erosion/flood works in §8c–d: micro-catchments, one-rock/check dams and branch piles (a proven low-cost Ebro technique) concentrate the scarce rain onto the planting lines. Reserve irrigation for the establishment year in the harshest spots only.
- **Local provenance.** Source seed and cuttings from the Ebro/Monegros so stock is pre-adapted and genetic integrity is kept.
- **Right plant, right band.** No woody planting on salt flats or bare gypsum crust; those are for halophytes and gypsophytes, or for protected natural colonisation.

## Caveats / to confirm with Sonya + teacher
- Palette is a verified regional shortlist, not a site survey. A quick field/orthophoto check of what already grows along the Valcuerna will refine it (and reveal existing seed sources).
- Frost limits on *Pistacia lentiscus*, *Nerium oleander* at the continental winter extremes; treat as marginal.
- Exact seed-collection months vary by species and year; the calendar gives the working window, not per-species dates.
