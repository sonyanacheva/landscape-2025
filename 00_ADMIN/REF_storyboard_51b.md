# §5.1b — Lynx-path storyboard (viewpoints along the corridor)
_17 viewpoints sampled every ~6 km along the 93 km least-cost corridor (Valcuerna ↔ Alcubierre), classified by what the lynx encounters. Data: `03_PROCESSED/viewpoints_51b.fgb` (+ `00_ADMIN/viewpoints_51b.csv`). Feeds the ChatGPT section-perspective renders (S + ChatGPT). Point colours in QGIS: red = barrier, blue = river, green star = protected core, olive = ecotone/cover, orange = discontinuity._

## The journey (what the map argues)
The modelled path **threads between the hard barriers** — it avoids every motorway and the high-speed rail, crossing only **one canal (V5) and one main road (V12)** in 93 km. It runs cover-to-cover through scrub/ecotone, links a chain of **protected cores** in the middle (V9–V11) and at the Valcuerna end (V15–V17). That is the story: the corridor exists, but it hangs on two crossing points and the continuity of the ecotone between cores.

## The 6 views worth rendering (section-perspectives)
1. **V5 — canal crossing:** lynx at a bare concrete canal; proposed vegetated crossing ramp. Arid, midday, sparse scrub.
2. **V7 — river crossing:** narrow riparian thread (Cinca/Alcanadre) in dryland; a green stepping-stone in ochre fields.
3. **V9 — steppe core:** open gypsum/steppe (Monegros), *Lygeum* + scrub mounds, a rabbit warren (prey base) — "visited, not lived-in" made visible.
4. **V12 — road crossing:** the N-road pinch point; lynx hesitating at the verge; proposed underpass.
5. **V13 — matrix hedgerow:** dryland-arable edge with a new hedgerow along a cañada / field corner — the intervention that stitches cover.
6. **V17 — Valcuerna arrival:** the dry barranco reactivated — xeroriparian planting, arto/*Ziziphus* mounds, the ecological spine as destination.
