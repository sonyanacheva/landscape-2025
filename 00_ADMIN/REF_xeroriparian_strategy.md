## Thesis
Not a lush/continuous riparian forest — a **xeroriparian belt**: latent ecological infrastructure that connects fragmented land uses and **catalyses animal-driven regeneration**. Success = "connectivity, persistence, feedback — not visual abundance." The ravine is a **sediment-driven system, not water-dominated**. Fauna are **co-designers** (disperse seed, perforate soil, make paths + humidity pockets). → Matches Sonya's arid-honest, feedback-loop vision exactly.

## Tactics (minimal, precise, temporary → escalating human→fauna)
Low stone lines + woody debris · micro-terracing + shallow depressions · **selective shrub planting "in islands, not belts"** · soft paths aligned to animal movement · monitoring (reinforce wins, remove failures) · **floods = "verification events, not failures"** (a memory layer) · light tourism (trekking on animal routes, MTB on robust ground, birdwatching at edges, renovated huts as observation nodes).
